The Need You Know It Is a Letting Light

Skills Exercised: Copyediting, Rewriting, Proofreading, Grammar Conventions

Goal: To revise the event description for "The Need You Know It Is a Letting Light" on the Prospect Park Alliance website to improve clarity, voice, and flow without compromising the intended message.

The first paragraph provides general information on the installation, while the next describes the piece and it's deeper messsage. Below are before and after images of the revised content. To create the after image, I used chrome developer tools to edit the text on the website.

Before


After


To complete this project I assessed each sentence to identify:

  • Sentence type (simple, compound, complex, compound-complex)
  • Verbs (tense, active/passive, strength)
  • Sentence structure

In my assessment, I found that some sentences were overly complex, making it difficult to follow main ideas. Out of the six total sentences, I copyedited five. In the following section, I include a side-by-side comparison of each sentence and a breakdown of changes made.

Sentence-by-Sentence Breakdown


Sentence 1

BRIC and Prospect Park Alliance in partnership with NYC Parks’ Art in the Parks Program present a new installation by Sarah E. Brook at the Lena Horne Bandshell at Prospect Park.

In partnership with NYC Parks’ Art in the Parks program, BRIC and Prospect Park Alliance unveil a new installation by artist Sarah E. Brook at the Lena Horne Bandshell.

  1. Moved "in partnership" to the beginning of the sentence to improve flow and clarity
  2. Changed "present" to "unveil" to make the sentence more dynamic and exciting
  3. Removed "at Prospect Park" to avoid sounding repetitive, since the website makes the location of the art installation clear

Sentence 2

The Need You Know It Is A Letting Light is a set of three abstract wooden sculptures and an accompanying mural that expands the artist’s exploration of communication between external and internal psychic space.

The Need You Know It Is A Letting Light is a set of three abstract wooden sculptures and a mural. Both pieces expand on the overarching focus of the artist‘s work: to explore how our external and internal psychic space communicate.

  1. Split into two sentences (simple and complex) so general description is seperate from the artist's vision to improve clarity
  2. Removed "accompanying" and changed verbs "exploration" and "communication" to their present tense for a more active voice
  3. Clarified that artist's work is being "expanded" on and added colon to highlight the focus of the artist's work for additional context and improved readability

Sentence 3

The mural’s colors of red, yellow, and green are sourced from the natural beauty of Prospect Park, and bothdraws viewers in from the park and radiates out from the Bandshell and stage into the park, reflecting thefunction of a bandshell to expand voices.

The mural's vibrant colors, inspired by Prospect Park's natural beauty, capture the attention of park visitors. Much like how the Bandshell directs sound from the stage into the park, the mural radiates outwards; mirroring the Bandshell's power to expand voices.

  1. Split into two sentences (simple and complex-compound) for readability and clarity
  2. Removed list of colors from the first sentence and changed wording to "park visitors" for improved clarity
  3. Removed to be verb "are sourced" and added stronger stronger, more appropiate verbs like "capture" and "directs"
  4. Used comparative phrase "much like" as the beginning of second sentence to make similarity between the bandshell and mural clearer

Sentence 4

The repurposed wood sculptures respond to the mural’s colors, expressing warm-hued shadows that appear to be held within.

In contrast to the mural, the wood sculptures express our inner world. When both pieces interact, they reveal the warm-hued shadows held within.

  1. Split into two sentences (simple and complex) to properly introduce the wood sculptures and the purpose they serve
  2. Added transitional phrase "in contrast to" at the beginning of the sentence for flow
  3. Removed "expressing" and "appear to be" for stronger, more active verbs like "express", "reveal", and "interact" that make connection between mural and wooden sculptures clearer

Sentence 5

The sculptures’ material communicate their singularity through wear-- a crooked nail, an empty hole, a worn edge-- and in their gently leaning formation, the sculptures can be read as bodies supporting one another.

Crafted from repurposed wood, each sculpture communicates it‘s singularity through wear— a crooked nail, an empty hole, a worn edge. Nevertheless, they gently lean on one another for support.

  1. Split into two sentences (complex and simple) to make the purpose of each piece individually and as a collective more clear
  2. Incorporated "repurposed" from previous sentence because here the materials are discussed more in-depth
  3. Began second sentence with the signpost "nevertheless" to introduce a new, contrasting idea
  4. Removed interpretation of art installation at the end to leave room for interpretation for readers
