Project Overview

Skills Exercised: Copywriting, Branding, Graphic Design, Health Literacy, Research

Goal: To communicate the benefits of maintaining personal health records to patients and introduce them to MyChart, an online patient portal.

Background: I first completed this project back in Spring 2023, during my final semester at the Borough of Manhattan Community College. Since then, I have learned a lot about style guides and communication across different visual mediums, so I decided to revisit this project over a year later. This page includes each completed part of the project accompanied by a short description of my process.

Completed Project

Presentation

This presentation was created using a template from NYC Health + Hospitals. I added the Bellevue logo at the top left corner of each slide, and created graphics for slides 5 through 8 in Canva (based on a similiar graphic used in the template). After researching what a personal health record (PHR) is and why it is important, I thought of how I could communicate this information to my audience. Firstly, I considered who would benefit from keeping a PHR. Then, I tried to think of the kinds of questions my audience would ask, like where they can start a PHR and how.

Brochure

To create an effective brochure, I researched other NYC Health + Hospitals brochures, such as this one from Gotham Health. Not only did this brochure help me adapt branding consistent with NYC Health + Hospitals promotional materials, but it informed the structure and content I chose to include. Since a brochure has less space than a presentation, I had to consider what information was the most important. That is, how I could effectively "sell" the idea through text alone without overwhelming the audience.

One pager

I recently created this one pager in Canva for patients who want to start a paper-based PHR. Since the main priority was introducing patients to MyChart in the original project, I thought this one pager made a nice addition. Like the other two parts of the project, I tried to think of what questions my audience may have and how I can break down the process.
